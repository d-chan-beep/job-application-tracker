When to Follow Up

  • 7 days after applying Send a professional follow-up email to confirm receipt and reiterate interest in the position.
  • 3 days after interview Send a thank-you message, reference something specific from the interview, and restate your qualifications.
  • 14 days, no response Send a final follow-up. If still no reply, document it and shift focus to other active applications.

Follow-Up Email Template

Subject: Follow-Up on Application - [Position Title] Dear Hiring Manager, I am writing to follow up on my application for the [Position Title] role at [Company Name], submitted on [Date Applied]. I remain very interested in this opportunity and am confident that my clinical background and experience align closely with your team's needs. Please let me know if you require any additional information or materials to support my application. I look forward to the possibility of speaking with you. Thank you for your time and consideration. Sincerely, [Your Name]

Phone Follow-Up Script

"Hello, my name is [Your Name]. I am calling to follow up on an application I submitted for the [Position Title] position at [Company Name]. I applied on [Date Applied] and wanted to confirm that my materials were received and to express my continued interest in the role. I understand you are likely reviewing many applications. I would welcome the chance to speak further about how my experience could be a strong fit for your team. Is there a good time we could connect, or a preferred contact I should reach out to? Thank you very much for your time. I look forward to hearing from you."

Professional Tips

DO

  • Research the correct contact person before reaching out
  • Keep messages brief and to the point
  • Show genuine continued interest in the role
  • Reference specific qualifications that match the job
  • Follow up by email first, then phone if needed

DO NOT

  • Call more than twice without a response
  • Use a pushy or demanding tone at any point
  • Apologize for following up - it is expected and professional
  • Send follow-ups more frequently than advised
  • Badmouth other employers or previous roles
